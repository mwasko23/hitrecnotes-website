The backstory

I watched ten people try to
download a file. Then I built this.

It started on a Teams call. I was showing my colleagues a voice-to-text tool — something I thought would help everyone write faster. Ten minutes, quick setup, done.

Ten minutes became an hour. People couldn't find the download. Someone's antivirus flagged it. Two people needed admin permissions they didn't have. We spent most of the call squinting at each other's screens trying to troubleshoot. And the best part? Nobody ever used that tool. Not a single person.

Here's what bugged me after: the technology works. Speaking is three to four times faster than typing. The AI behind it is incredibly accurate. But every tool I found was either too complicated to set up, too expensive to justify, or locked behind a monthly subscription that nobody wanted to add to an already-ridiculous stack of recurring charges.

So I built HitRecNotes. One app. One hotkey. Hold it, speak, release — your words appear wherever your cursor is. Emails, docs, Slack, whatever you're working in. It costs $39, once, and it's yours. No subscription. No usage limits. No asking your manager to approve another monthly charge.

I've been using it every day for months now. It's one of those tools that sounds small until you realize you haven't typed a single email since lunch.

— Marta, builder of HitRecNotes

How does the setup work?

After you buy HitRecNotes, you'll connect it to the AI that powers the transcription — the same technology behind ChatGPT's voice features. It takes about 5 minutes, and we have a step-by-step guide with screenshots. Or, if you'd prefer, you can book a free setup call and we'll walk you through it together.

Do I have to pay monthly for the transcription?

No. There's a small usage cost that goes directly to the AI provider — most people spend under $1 per month. You load a one-time credit of $5 to $10, and that lasts most people months. There is no subscription, ever.

How accurate is it?

Very. HitRecNotes uses the same AI that powers ChatGPT's voice features. It handles accents, natural speech patterns, and even adds punctuation automatically. Most users see around 97% accuracy right out of the box — no training period needed.

Does it work offline?

No. HitRecNotes needs an internet connection to reach the AI that does the transcription. Most people who use it are already at a desk with Wi-Fi, so this isn't usually an issue.

What about my privacy?

Your audio goes directly to the AI for transcription and is immediately deleted. HitRecNotes never sees, stores, or has access to your audio or text. No screenshots. No tracking. No telemetry. Your work stays yours.

Can I get a refund?

Yes. 30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked.

Windows only for now. Mac support is in development.

Why use this instead of WisprFlow, Otter, or the built-in Windows dictation?

Most voice-to-text tools charge $10 to $17 per month — that's $120 to $200 a year. HitRecNotes is $39, once. It's more accurate than built-in Windows dictation, works in every app, and pastes text right where your cursor is. And unlike some competitors, it doesn't capture screenshots of your screen or store your audio.

Does it save my recordings?

No. Audio is processed, transcribed, and immediately discarded. Nothing is saved to your computer or to any server.
